## 1. SLIDE DECK PROMPT

You are an expert presentation designer and visual storyteller creating a haunting, elegiac, unflinching slide deck for a poetry/quotes piece about authenticity and the systems that punish it.

**PROJECT CONTEXT**
- Topic: Waking Up to What We've Built — a poem and essay about the civilizational punishment of authentic people
- Tone: Unflinching, elegiac, still
- Audience: Creators who've watched honest work underperform polished performance; people who've refused to wear the mask and paid for it; anyone who's felt the specific sting of watching hollow confidence get praised while genuine effort gets dismantled

**VISUAL DIRECTION**
- Style: Fine art photography meets museum-quality literary design. Think a Criterion Collection opening sequence — slow, deliberate, visually precise. Not a poetry slam aesthetic. Not Instagram inspirational. The mood of a late Tarkovsky film: stillness that contains enormous pressure. Every slide is a frame you'd hang.
- Color Palette: Deep indigo `#1C1635` · Antique gold `#C9A96E` · Cream `#FAF3E0` · Dusty rose `#D4A5A5` · Soft charcoal `#3D3D4F`
- Typography: Primary — refined literary serif (think Freight Display or Playfair Display), generous line spacing, no tight tracking. Poem lines in italic, generous leading. Essay lines in regular weight. Attribution in small caps. Headlines large and cold. NO bold grotesque. NO display fonts that feel motivational.
- Imagery Direction: Cracked porcelain on cold stone. Empty mirrors. Hands pressing other hands down. A crowd in blur, one face sharp. A figure at a pale window, back to camera. Raking light from one side only — never warm, never golden, never welcoming. Shadows with hard edges. The aesthetic of a slow documentary about grief, not a self-help retreat.
- Transitions: Long cross-dissolves, 800ms minimum. No slide-ins. No zooms. Each transition feels like a page turning in an old book — reluctant, weighted.

**HERO SLIDE**
- Title: Waking Up to What We've Built
- Main Text: THE END OF BEING FOOLED
- Sub Text: A poem about authenticity, and the world that punishes it
- Hero Visual: A cracked white porcelain mask lying face-up on cold grey concrete, single raking light source from the left creating a deep shadow in the fracture line. The crack runs through center — revealing only more white, nothing inside. Soft vignette at edges. No hands. No face. Just the object and the floor and the light. Color grade: desaturated, cool, slightly lifted blacks so no pure crush. Overlay: deep indigo `#1C1635` at 45% opacity top half only, so the mask and floor breathe at bottom. Title "Waking Up to What We've Built" in cream `#FAF3E0` literary serif, large, top-left aligned, generous tracking. Sub text in antique gold `#C9A96E` small caps below, 14pt equivalent.

**SLIDE STRUCTURE + CONTENT**

SLIDE 1 — HERO
- Visual: Cracked porcelain mask on concrete as described above
- Heading: Waking Up to What We've Built
- Text: "A poem about authenticity / And the world that punishes it / By Tarun Gupta"
- Layout: Title top-left. Author attribution bottom-left in small antique gold caps. Image fills full bleed behind deep indigo overlay.

SLIDE 2 — THE MOMENT IT CAME FROM
- Visual: Blurred crowd all facing one direction — one face in sharp focus center frame, looking directly at camera, expression unreadable. Monochrome treatment with a slight cool blue push. High contrast. The in-focus face lit from above and slightly to the left.
- Heading: The Moment It Came From
- Bullets rendered as three staggered lines, each on its own line, not list-style:
  "Watching someone get torn apart for being real."
  "Watching the hollow ones get praised."
  "Not rage — clarity."
- Layout: Text left-aligned over dark indigo panel occupying left 40%. Image bleeds right 60%.

SLIDE 3 — THE POEM (full text slide)
- Visual: Aged cream paper texture, barely visible — like parchment under low directional light. No photography. The slide is the poem.
- Background: Cream `#FAF3E0` with subtle paper grain texture at low opacity
- Poem rendered in full, italic literary serif, deep indigo `#1C1635`, generous line spacing (1.8 leading):

  *Waking up to a realization*
  *About how the world is changing*
  *Thoughts driven by exteriority*
  *No care about what lies within*
  *People drooling over aesthetics*
  *Shitting all over the hearts of the good ones*
  *Praising the false gods*
  *Demeaning deserving humans*
  *Talking about love and life*
  *Not knowing the true meaning*
  *How our civilization has turned out*
  *wearing layers of masks themselves*
  *ready to tear down the true faces*
  *Only to make themselves happy*
  *Only to make themselves happy*

- The repeated final line gets its own visual weight: same size but the second instance has a slightly longer left indent — a typographic breath, a repetition that isn't redundant.
- Attribution below: "— Tarun Gupta" in antique gold small caps.
- NO other visual elements. The poem is the design.

SLIDE 4 — THE MASK PARADOX
- Visual: Empty mirror in a dimly lit room — the reflection shows a standing figure, but no one is visible in the actual room. Just the mirror and the dark behind it. Shot with practical ambient light only. No fill lights. Deep shadows. Dusty rose `#D4A5A5` cast from an unseen warm source at frame edge.
- Heading: The Mask Paradox
- Lines (not bullets — rendered as standalone sentences with space between):
  "The masked aren't confident — they're defended."
  "Authenticity indicts performance."
  "The mask needs the real face to disappear."
- Layout: Deep indigo panel right 45%. Text in cream on dark. Image left 55%.

SLIDE 5 — WHAT THIS DOES TO CREATORS
- Visual: A desk at night — single lamp, notebook open, pen down, no hands visible. Warm amber from the lamp but cool darkness everywhere else. The tension of two light temperatures. Shot from slightly above, looking down at 30 degrees. Quiet but not peaceful.
- Heading: What This Does to Creators
- Lines:
  "The internet says it wants authenticity."
  "The numbers often tell a different story."
  "The costume grows faster than the body of work."
  "Every creator must choose which one to build."
- Layout: Full bleed image, dark overlay `#1C1635` at 55%. Text bottom-left, cream, large literary serif for the final line, smaller for preceding lines. The final line gets extra size — it's the decision, not the observation.

SLIDE 6 — THE WAKING UP
- Visual: Person seated at a large window, back to camera, pale grey light coming through. Complete stillness — no implied motion, no resolution, no action. Just presence. The window takes up two-thirds of the frame. The figure is small. The world outside the window is overexposed — white, formless.
- Heading: The Waking Up
- Lines:
  "The poem doesn't offer a fix."
  "It offers a name."

**DESIGN RULES**
1. Never use pure black or pure white — deep indigo and cream are your anchors
2. Imagery is always desaturated or near-monochrome — let color exist in typography and overlays only
3. Every slide has at most one active visual element. No collages.
4. Line breaks in poem text are sacred — never reflow for layout convenience
5. Antique gold `#C9A96E` is used only for attribution, pullquotes, and the final emotional beat of any slide — never decoratively
6. The repeated line "Only to make themselves happy" must always appear at different visual weight than surrounding text — larger, or with more surrounding space

**OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS**
6 slides total. 16:9 widescreen format. Canva-compatible layout. Export-ready at 1920×1080. No slide numbers visible in final output. No branded footer except "Tarun Gupta" or "@breathofpoetry" in antique gold small caps, bottom-left, 10pt equivalent.

## 2. INSTAGRAM STORY SEQUENCE PROMPT

Create a cinematic fine-art Instagram Story sequence for a poetry/quotes post titled: "Waking Up to What We've Built"

**THE EMOTIONAL CORE**
The viewer should feel the cold clarity of someone who has stopped being confused — who has finally named the thing that's been wearing them down. Not catharsis. Not relief. The specific stillness of seeing something clearly for the first time and realizing you cannot unsee it.

**STYLE DIRECTION**
Tarkovsky-slow, museum-quiet. Every frame is a painting you'd stop in front of. Photography is near-monochrome — desaturated with cool blue-grey push, occasional dusty rose bleed at frame edges. No lifestyle warmth. No golden hour. The light is overhead or raking — it reveals, it doesn't flatter. Typography appears slowly, white on dark, as if typed by someone who is choosing each word deliberately. No motion graphics. No kinetic text. The stillness IS the message. Transitions are long cross-dissolves, never cuts. The sequence breathes.

**COLOR PALETTE**
Deep indigo `#1C1635` · Antique gold `#C9A96E` · Cream `#FAF3E0` · Dusty rose `#D4A5A5` · Soft charcoal `#3D3D4F`

**FORMAT**
Vertical 9:16. 7 frames. Cross-dissolve transitions at 700ms. Typography: refined literary serif, italic for poem lines, regular weight for essay lines. No bold. No decorative elements.

**STORY FLOW**

FRAME 1:
- Visual: Pure deep indigo `#1C1635` background. Absolute black screen with a barely-visible paper grain texture. No image. Silence before sound.
- Text: A single word, cream `#FAF3E0`, literary serif, 52pt, centered: *Notice.*
- The period is load-bearing. Hold 3 seconds before dissolve.

FRAME 2:
- Visual: Blurred crowd in motion — all bodies moving in one direction, one figure center-frame in sharp focus, face turned directly at camera. Expression: not angry, not sad. Unreadable. High contrast. Desaturated. Cool blue push on shadows.
- Text (upper third, cream): *Thoughts driven by exteriority.*
- Sub-text (lower fifth, antique gold `#C9A96E`, smaller): *No care about what lies within.*
- Typography italic serif. Text over image with deep indigo `#1C1635` gradient top 30% for legibility.

FRAME 3:
- Visual: Close-up of an ornate white ceramic mask, face-up on cold grey stone floor. Single raking light from left edge creates deep shadow in the crack running through its center. No hands. No face. Just the object, the floor, and the fracture. Color: almost monochrome, a hint of dusty rose `#D4A5A5` in the warm areas of the ceramic.
- Text (center, large): *wearing layers of masks themselves*
- Lower text (smaller, cream): *ready to tear down the true faces*
- Both lines in italic serif. Generous space between them.

FRAME 4:
- Visual: A single open hand extended toward camera, palm up — light from above, cool and clinical. A second hand enters slowly from right frame edge and presses the first hand down and out of shot. Deliberate. Slow. The frame holds on the empty space where the hand was.
- Text appears only after the hand is gone. Center frame, cream, large:
  *shitting all over the hearts of the good ones*
- No softening. No adjustment. Exact line from poem.

FRAME 5:
- Visual: Empty mirror in a dimly lit room. The reflection shows a standing figure in silhouette. No one visible in the actual room — just the mirror and the dark behind it. Dusty rose `#D4A5A5` ambient glow from off-frame right. Near-monochrome except for that one warm-cool bleed.
- Text, appearing in two beats (dissolve between):
  Beat 1: *Only to make themselves happy.*
  Beat 2 (same frame, text shifts slightly right, one beat pause): *Only to make themselves happy.*
- Second instance should be slightly larger — the repetition is structural, not decorative.

FRAME 6:
- Visual: Same mirror, same room. The reflection is now gone. Just the empty glass and the dark room behind it. No figure anywhere. The absence is the content.
- Text (lower third, antique gold `#C9A96E`, italic):
  *The mask doesn't just hide — it needs to.*
- No other text.

FRAME 7:
- Visual: A person seated at a large window, back to camera, pale grey light flooding through. Figure is small — the window is vast. Outside: overexposed, formless white. Complete stillness. No motion implied. No resolution offered.
- Text (center, cream, large, italic):
  *The waking up isn't despair.*
  *It's the end of being fooled.*
- Below, in antique gold small caps, very small: TARUN GUPTA · @BREATHOFPOETRY
- Hold longer than other frames — 4 seconds minimum before sequence ends.

**ANIMATION STYLE**
Text fades in on each frame over 600ms, no motion. No typewriter effect. No slide-in. Text appears as if it was always there and only now visible. Cross-dissolve between all frames.

**TYPOGRAPHY**
Refined literary serif throughout. Italic for all poem lines. Regular weight for essay/commentary lines. No headline weight. No grotesque. Cream `#FAF3E0` on dark frames. Deep indigo `#1C1635` if ever on light background (Frame 1 only). Antique gold for attribution and the one direct-address line only.

**GOAL**
The viewer reaches the last frame in a state of quiet recognition — not activated, not inspired to share, not motivated. Simply still. The feeling of having been seen in something they've never had words for. That stillness is the conversion event.

## 3. REEL COVER / THUMBNAIL PROMPT

Create a cinematic vertical reel cover (9:16) for a poetry piece about authenticity and the systems that punish it — titled "Waking Up to What We've Built."

**VISUAL DIRECTION**
A close-up of a cracked white porcelain mask lying face-up on cold grey concrete. Shot from slightly above, angled at 25 degrees — not directly overhead, not eye-level. A single raking light source enters from the left edge, throwing a sharp shadow into the fracture line that runs through the center of the mask. The crack reveals only more white ceramic underneath — nothing inside, no depth, just more surface. The mask is ornate: delicate raised features, classical styling, the kind you'd see in a Venetian carnival context — but stripped of all color and context and placed on the most ordinary surface possible. That contrast is the image. Surrounding floor: dark grey concrete, slightly wet near the bottom edge. A barely-perceptible ring of dusty rose `#D4A5A5` ambient light halos the mask — as if it was once alive. Color grade: desaturated to near-monochrome, cool blue push in shadows, blacks lifted to `#2A2A3A` so nothing fully crushes. Subtle vignette — darkest at top corners, lightest at the mask itself.

**TEXT OVERLAY**
- Main text: THE ONES WHO REFUSED TO WEAR IT
- Font: Refined literary serif, regular weight, cream `#FAF3E0`, all caps, 46pt equivalent, centered, upper third of the frame — above the mask, not over it
- Sub text: A poem · Tarun Gupta
- Font: Same serif, small caps, antique gold `#C9A96E`, 14pt equivalent, bottom center — below the mask

**COLOR TREATMENT**
Background: Near-monochrome with cool blue-grey push. The mask itself holds the lightest point in the frame — eye goes there first, then reads upward to headline. Deep indigo `#1C1635` semi-transparent gradient occupies top 25% for text legibility. No solid color panels. No gradients except this utility overlay at top.

**TYPOGRAPHY**
Main headline: literary serif, tracked slightly wide (+50 tracking), centered, no bold. Sub-attribution: small caps, no tracking adjustment, antique gold. The weight contrast between headline cream and gold attribution creates the hierarchy. Nothing else.

**MOOD**
The specific cold recognition of seeing something for what it is — beauty that was always performance, and the fracture that finally shows there was nothing underneath.

**OPTIMIZATION NOTE**
The cracked mask as primary visual is a scroll-stopper in a vertical poetry feed because it violates expectation: most poetry reels use nature, light, or human faces. A single object on concrete reads as deliberate, strange, and arresting. The headline "THE ONES WHO REFUSED TO WEAR IT" creates an in-group identity hook — the viewer either immediately self-identifies or is curious enough to tap. The composition leaves deliberate empty space at top and bottom to avoid crop interference from Reels UI chrome.
