## 1. SLIDE DECK PROMPT

You are an expert presentation designer and visual storyteller creating a contemplative, intimate slide deck for a poetry essay about real love.

PROJECT CONTEXT
Topic: "Safe and Alive: Why Love Requires Both, Not Either"
Tone: Tender, vulnerable, awakened
Audience: People in deep relationships seeking validation that real love doesn't demand compromise between safety and growth; poetry and philosophy readers; those questioning whether they're in the right relationship

VISUAL DIRECTION
Style: Fine art photography meets poetic minimalism. Every slide is a single emotional frame — not a presentation, but a visual poem. Golden-hour and candlelit aesthetics dominating. Generous negative space. Handcrafted textures. Museum-quality restraint. Motion feels like pages turning in an old book.

Color Palette: Deep indigo #1C1635 (background/depth), Antique gold #C9A96E (accents, warmth), Cream #FAF3E0 (text, breathing room), Dusty rose #D4A5A5 (emotional moments, subtle color lift), Soft charcoal #3D3D4F (secondary text, grounding)

Typography: Elegant serif (Garamond or similar) for poem lines in italic. Refined sans-serif (Montserrat or similar) for headers in small caps. Generous line spacing (1.8-2.0) between poem lines. Body text in cream or soft charcoal against deep indigo. Every text block has breathing room — whitespace is intentional.

Imagery Direction: Couples under blankets in low, warm light. Candlelight on skin. Closed eyes of someone at rest. Wide-open eyes of someone awake. Hands holding. Family tables with integrated circles. Aged paper with handwriting. Soft bokeh of lights in darkness. No modern tech. No bright environments. No stock photography of smiling people. Film-grain texture subtle throughout.

Animation/Transitions: Long, slow dissolves (2-3 seconds between slides). Subtle pan across textures in hero slides. No fast cuts. Motion that breathes. Silence held between transitions. As if turning pages of an illuminated manuscript.

HERO SLIDE
Title: "Safe and Alive"
Main Text: "THE MYTH OF CHOOSING BETWEEN THEM"
Sub Text: "Real love asks for both at once. Not compromise. Integration."
Hero Visual Direction: Close-up of two people's faces under a cream-colored blanket in near-total darkness. Warm candlelight from below illuminating their skin. One person's eyes closed (safe). One person's eyes open (alive). Soft focus. Grain texture. The image is intimate enough that viewers feel like voyeurs.

SLIDE STRUCTURE + CONTENT

Slide 1: Title Slide
- Small caps header: "Safe and Alive"
- Cream text on deep indigo background
- Subheader in dusty rose: "Why Love Requires Both, Not Either"
- Visual: The blanket/candlelight scene at full scale. Quiet. Holds for 3 seconds before dissolve to next slide.

Slide 2: The Problem with Safety Alone
- Header (small caps): "Safety Alone"
- Poem lines in italic, centered, generous spacing:
  "you stop growing
   you become smaller
   protection becomes a cage"
- Visual: Single hand resting on aged paper. Candlelight casting the hand's shadow on cream background. Motion: slow pan left across the shadow, revealing more negative space. No other elements.

Slide 3: The Problem with Aliveness Alone
- Header (small caps): "Aliveness Alone"
- Poem lines in italic:
  "excitement without roots
   you burn bright and burn out
   no foundation to build on"
- Visual: Bokeh of candlelight and flames slightly out of focus, golden and warm, but unstable and diffuse. No clear point of focus. Motion: gentle fade in, then slow zoom out to reveal the chaos within the blur.

Slide 4: When Both Exist Together
- Header (small caps): "Both at Once"
- Quote in italic, cream, centered with maximum whitespace:
  "your nervous system relaxes
   your presence expands
   you stop performing"
- Visual: Two people, one face relaxed (eyes closed), one face awake (eyes open), overlaid slightly or side-by-side. Candlelight warm. The visual shows the same person or couple embodying both states simultaneously. Soft focus on faces. Deep indigo background.

Slide 5: The Moment of Integration
- Header (small caps): "Integration"
- Key lines from poem in italic:
  "I melted right then and there
   when you talk about her
   The twinkle in your eyes"
- Visual: The beloved sitting at a family table with the viewer's mama. Both laughing or in deep conversation. Warm natural or candlelight. The viewer is watching from just outside frame. Emotional stillness. This is the moment the poem describes.

Slide 6: Dissolution, Not Conquest
- Header (small caps): "Dissolution"
- Final poem lines in italic, cream, generous spacing:
  "then dissolving in my arms
   out of sheer love and embarrassment"
- Visual: Return to the blanket and darkness. Both people visible now, eyes open, holding each other. The candlelight is slightly brighter — not triumphant, but softer. Complete presence. No performance.

Slide 7: The Takeaway
- Header (small caps): "Real Love"
- Key statement in italic:
  "Stop choosing between safety and growth.
   Stop dividing yourself.
   Demand integration, not compromise."
- Visual: Wide shot of a family circle — mama, beloved, viewer, others. All sitting together. Candlelit dinner or living room. Everyone at rest and awake at once. Integration visible as physical closeness and emotional presence. Soft, grainy, warm.

DESIGN RULES
1. Every slide is a single emotional moment, not an information dump. Text never exceeds 3 lines per slide.
2. Imagery is always specific (not symbolic). Real people, real candlelight, real fabric, real hands.
3. Negative space is protected — whitespace on every slide equals 40%+ of the slide area.
4. Typography hierarchy is extreme: poem lines in italic serif at large scale. Headers in small caps, secondary text minimal.
5. Color palette stays consistent: deep indigo dominates backgrounds. Cream and gold are highlights. Dusty rose appears in exactly 1-2 emotional peaks.
6. Motion is contemplative: no transition faster than 2 seconds. Dissolves only. No wipes, no bounces, no effects.

OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS
This deck is intended for people who question whether they're asking too much from a relationship. Each slide should make them feel seen, not lectured. The poem is the authority here, not the presenter. Use the poem's language exactly. Let the pauses and line breaks in the poem inform slide breaks and whitespace.

## 2. INSTAGRAM STORY SEQUENCE PROMPT

Create a cinematic intimate Instagram Story sequence for a poetry post titled "Safe and Alive: The Moment You Stop Dividing Yourself."

THE EMOTIONAL CORE
When you imagine the person you love belonging everywhere you belong — with your family, in your sacred spaces, integrated into your entire life — and you realize that the moment is already here. That's the moment you stop protecting them from yourself and start dissolving into the reality of being fully known.

STYLE DIRECTION
Visual language: Candlelit intimate moments. Soft bokeh. Film grain throughout. No modern tech, no bright colors, no performance. Every frame feels stolen — voyeuristic, tender, real. Pacing is slow and deliberate; let viewers feel the silence between frames. The story unfolds like a poem: opening image establishes mood, middle frames build emotional tension through specificity (candlelight on skin, eyes meeting, family integration), final frames dissolve into stillness and acceptance.

Color Palette: Deep indigo #1C1635 (night, depth), Antique gold #C9A96E (candlelight warmth), Cream #FAF3E0 (skin, text), Dusty rose #D4A5A5 (emotional peak only), Soft charcoal #3D3D4F (text on light backgrounds)

FORMAT
Vertical 9:16, 8 frames, transitions: long dissolves (2-second hold minimum per frame before auto-advance)

STORY FLOW

FRAME 1:
Visual: Close-up of a blanket — cream, soft, textured — in darkness. Candlelight flickering at the edge of frame, creating warm bokeh. The blanket fills most of the frame. Pure texture. Grainy, soft focus.
Text: "Safe"
Animation: Slow fade in. Hold 3 seconds.

FRAME 2:
Visual: Extreme close-up of two people's faces under the blanket. One person's eyes are closed (peaceful, safe). Candlelight on their skin. Breath visible in cool air. Intimate, as if you're lying between them.
Text: (none)
Animation: Dissolve. Hold 2 seconds.

FRAME 3:
Visual: Same framing. The eyes of the person who was closed are now open, wide awake, fully present. Intensity. Recognition. Same candlelight, same intimacy, different state.
Text: "Alive"
Animation: Dissolve. Hold 3 seconds.

FRAME 4:
Visual: Both people visible now. Eyes meeting. Hands touching. Vulnerability visible on both faces. Candlelight dancing between them. The image says: this is what both at once looks like.
Text: (none — the image speaks)
Animation: Dissolve. Hold 3 seconds.

FRAME 5:
Visual: Shift in setting. The beloved is sitting at a family table with the viewer's mama. Both are laughing or in deep conversation. Warm natural light or candlelight. The viewer watches from just outside the frame — you see this moment from their perspective.
Text: "When they belong"
Animation: Dissolve. Hold 3 seconds.

FRAME 6:
Visual: The viewer moves into frame now. Sitting between mama and beloved. All three together. Close enough that you feel the physical warmth. Integration visible as closeness. No one is performing.
Text: "Everywhere"
Animation: Dissolve. Hold 3 seconds.

FRAME 7:
Visual: Return to the blanket and candlelight. Both people now glowing softly. The darkness is complete around them, but they're luminous within it. Eyes open, present, completely at rest at the same time.
Text: "You stop dividing"
Animation: Dissolve. Hold 3 seconds.

FRAME 8:
Visual: The same scene, held longer. No movement. No text change. Just the image of two people, safe and alive, integrated into one moment. Candlelight steady. Grain texture. Museum quality. This is the final image — let it breathe.
Text: (fade to nothing)
Animation: Dissolve, slow. Hold 4 seconds. Natural completion — viewers can screenshot this frame.

ANIMATION STYLE
No music cue specified, but pacing should feel silent or with ambient sound (candlelight crackle, breath, fabric movement). Each frame holds for 2-3 seconds minimum. No fast cuts. Transitions are 1-second dissolves. The entire sequence should feel like 20-25 seconds of slow, meditative storytelling.

TYPOGRAPHY
Text appears in cream (#FAF3E0) or dusty rose (#D4A5A5) depending on background. Small caps or elegant serif italic. Centered or bottom-third placement. Minimal text — 1-2 words per frame maximum, except frame 6 which carries "everywhere" as the word that unlocks the insight.

GOAL
Viewer finishes the story feeling seen in their own desire for integration. Not longing, not yearning — recognition. The moment when all the parts of your life collapse into one circle and you can finally breathe. The final frame should make them want to send it to someone, or sit in silence with their own beloved.

## 3. REEL COVER / THUMBNAIL PROMPT

Create a cinematic vertical reel cover (9:16) for the poetry essay "Safe and Alive."

VISUAL DIRECTION
Extreme close-up of two people's faces lying next to each other on a cream or white pillow. One person's eyes are closed (absolute peace, surrender). The other person's eyes are open and looking directly at the camera — awake, present, alive. Candlelight or golden-hour light illuminates their skin from one side, creating a warm, intimate glow. The camera is close enough that you feel like you're the third person in the bed. Soft focus on the edges. Visible skin texture. Film grain throughout. The background should be pure darkness or blurred fabric (the blanket). This is not a styled photo shoot — it's a stolen moment of real intimacy.

TEXT OVERLAY
Main text: "SAFE AND ALIVE" (all caps, cream or antique gold, elegant serif or refined sans-serif, positioned top-third of cover, centered)
Sub text: "The moment you stop choosing" (optional, positioned center-frame in smaller italicized type, dusty rose or cream, elegant serif)

COLOR TREATMENT
Palette: Deep indigo #1C1635 shadows. Antique gold #C9A96E candlelight. Cream #FAF3E0 skin tones and text. Soft charcoal #3D3D4F for secondary text. Subtle warm color grade — no cool tones, no desaturation. The image feels like it's lit by candles and nothing else. Slight vignette darkening the edges to pull focus to the faces.

TYPOGRAPHY
Weight: Bold small caps for "SAFE AND ALIVE." Light or regular weight for sub text in italic serif.
Placement: Main text top-third, centered, 48-56pt. Sub text center of image, 20-24pt, italic.
Color: Cream (#FAF3E0) for main text. Dusty rose (#D4A5A5) for sub text to add emotional warmth.

MOOD
Vulnerable tenderness. The exact moment when you realize you don't need to choose — you can be held and be awake at the same time. Intimate without being performative. Soft without being weak. Present without trying.

OPTIMIZATION NOTE
In a vertical reel feed, the close-up of two faces creates immediate stop power. Viewers recognize intimacy instantly. The contrast between closed eyes (safety) and open eyes (aliveness) is visually arresting — it communicates the core tension in a single image. The candlelight and texture signal premium, literary content (not motivational). Text placement (top-third and center) doesn't interfere with the faces. The overall effect should make viewers pause and tap through to the full reel or blog post.
