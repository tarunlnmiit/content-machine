## 1. SLIDE DECK PROMPT

You are an expert presentation designer and visual storyteller creating a cinematic, emotionally honest slide deck for a Life & Self-Development essay on mental health stigma and the inherited lie about strength.

**PROJECT CONTEXT**
- Topic: The Lie We Inherited About Strength — mental health stigma, male silence, and redefining strength through honesty
- Tone: Honest, warm, quietly urgent
- Audience: Men in their late 20s–40s who've white-knuckled life longer than they should; people who've watched someone they love disappear into silence; anyone who's confused emotional suppression with strength

**VISUAL DIRECTION**
- Style: Warm cinematic photography meets editorial restraint. This is not a wellness brand deck — it's closer to a literary journal or a documentary film. Every slide should feel like a still frame someone paused on because they needed a moment. Generous negative space. Low ambient light. Human stillness. Emotional weight without melodrama.
- Color Palette: Warm beige `#F5F0E8` as the base field · Amber gold `#C9A96E` for accent lines, pull quotes, and slide numbers · Muted brown `#8B6F5E` for secondary text and texture overlays · Off-white `#FFFBF0` for hero text on dark slides · Soft black `#1A1A1A` for body copy on light slides
- Typography: Bold humanist sans-serif (e.g., Neue Haas Grotesk or DM Sans) at large scale for hero statements. Clean readable body type at moderate weight for bullet content. No decorative fonts. No script. Precision in hierarchy — headline 3× the size of body copy. Italic only for key quotes.
- Imagery Direction: Every image should feel like it was captured without the subject knowing — candid over posed. Specific scenes: a person alone at a kitchen table at 1 AM, one lamp, empty chair across from them. Two men on a bench in autumn light, not quite talking, one looking away. A hand on a forearm — not a hug, just contact. A cracked concrete wall with amber light bleeding through from the other side. No therapy-as-cliché imagery (no couches, no tissue boxes). No corporate wellness stock. No bright saturated environments.
- Animation/Transitions: Slow cross-dissolve between slides. No builds or click animations unless specified. Typography fades in at 70% opacity then settles. Imagery appears first, text follows 0.4s later. Pacing is deliberate — this is not a listicle deck, it's an essay.

**HERO SLIDE — SLIDE 1**
- Title: The Lie We Inherited About Strength
- Main Text (ALL CAPS, hero scale): BREAK A BONE. PEOPLE SHOW UP.
- Sub Text: "Say you're drowning. The room goes quiet."
- Hero Visual Direction: Split-tone composition. Left half: warm golden-hour light, a plaster arm cast with handwriting on it, half a smiling face just visible at the edge of frame. Right half: deep shadow, a person alone at a table, the cast gone, the warmth gone. The divide between the two halves is a soft gradient fade, not a hard line. Amber gold `#C9A96E` used as a thin horizontal line separating headline from subtitle. Off-white `#FFFBF0` type on dark right-side background. Slide number hidden on hero slide.

**SLIDE STRUCTURE + CONTENT**

SLIDE 2 — The Numbers Don't Lie
- Heading: "The numbers don't lie" (set in amber `#C9A96E`, moderate weight, sentence case)
- Bullets:
  - 40% of men won't talk about their mental health — not to friends, not to family, not to anyone.
  - Globally, men die by suicide at significantly higher rates than women.
  - This isn't weakness. It's a systemic failure to give people permission to struggle out loud.
- Visual: Dark background (`#1A1A1A`), statistics displayed as large typographic data — "40%" in off-white at massive scale, behind the bullet text as a ghost number. No chart. No infographic. Just the weight of the number itself as a visual element. Amber thin rule above heading.

SLIDE 3 — The Stigma Is Inherited, Not Chosen
- Heading: "The stigma is inherited, not chosen"
- Bullets:
  - "Real men don't cry."
  - "Therapy is for people who can't handle life."
  - These scripts ran so long they became wallpaper — nobody notices them anymore, but they're still running.
- Visual: Light background (`#F5F0E8`). Dusty, draped furniture in an old room — fabric-covered shapes in warm afternoon light. The kind of room where inherited things accumulate, unused but unexamined. Quotes rendered in italic, slightly smaller than the body bullets, indented — like overheard conversation.

SLIDE 4 — We Confuse Support with Problem-Solving
- Heading: "We confuse support with problem-solving"
- Bullets:
  - Someone calls because they need to be heard.
  - We reach for budgeting tools, action plans, and logic.
  - Some moments don't need a mechanic. They need a witness.
- Visual: Two people at a table — one leaning forward with a laptop open, expression fixed in focus-mode, solving. The other looking down and slightly away, arms folded, clearly not there for the spreadsheet. Warm indoor light. The image bleeds into the left third of the slide, text occupies the right two-thirds on warm beige `#F5F0E8`. Pull quote — "Some moments don't need a mechanic. They need a witness." — set in amber italic below bullets, separated by a thin rule.

SLIDE 5 — The Personal Trainer Double Standard
- Heading: "The personal trainer double standard"
- Bullets:
  - No stigma: hiring a personal trainer to get physically stronger.
  - Automatic stigma: hiring a therapist to get emotionally stronger.
  - Same logic. Completely different social response.
- Visual: Clean vertical split. Left panel: warm bright gym, open door, inviting light spilling out. Right panel: a closed door with frosted glass — same color palette, same composition, but the door is shut. Muted brown `#8B6F5E` overlay on both panels at 20% opacity to unify them. Final bullet — "Same logic. Completely different social response." — set in larger weight than the others, slight amber tint.

SLIDE 6 — Two Kinds of Strength (EMOTIONAL PEAK)
- Heading: "Two kinds of strength"
- Bullets:
  - Strength built on suppression looks solid — until the right pressure hits.
  - Strength built on honesty bends — but doesn't break.
  - Vulnerability isn't the opposite of strength. It's proof of it.
- Visual: Full-bleed dark background `#1A1A1A`. Two abstract material forms under identical visual pressure — one angular, fractured, brittle-looking; one curved, flexed but intact. Same size. Same amber-gold tone `#C9A96E`. No labels. The visual argument made without words. Final bullet set as a standalone pull quote at 1.4× body size, off-white `#FFFBF0`, generous negative space around it. This slide breathes longer than the others.

SLIDE 7 — What Breaking Silence Actually Looks Like
- Heading: "What breaking silence actually looks like"
- Bullets:
  - Ask how someone is — and actually wait for the real answer.
  - Listen without immediately pivoting to solutions.
  - Mention therapy without treating it like a last resort or a verdict.
- Visual: Light background. Close-up of two people in genuine conversation — one leaning in, face open, listening intently. Not staged. Candid warmth. Warm indoor lamp light at the edge of frame. Soft focus on the background. The heading rendered small and quiet — this slide should feel actionable and calm after the emotional peak of Slide 6.

SLIDE 8 — Closing (The Wall Cracks)
- Heading: "The wall cracks one conversation at a time"
- Bullets:
  - You don't have to carry your struggle alone.
  - Neither does the person sitting next to you.
  - Start there.
- Visual: A concrete wall photographed close — a single crack running from mid-frame downward, amber-warm light bleeding through from behind. The crack is not dramatic. It's small. It's enough. Slide uses full-bleed image with text overlaid in off-white `#FFFBF0`. Final bullet — "Start there." — set alone on its own line, smaller than the others, as if spoken quietly. Blog URL or handle in amber, very small, bottom-right.

**DESIGN RULES**
1. Maximum 30 words of copy per slide — if a bullet exceeds 18 words, break it across two lines with deliberate line breaks, not auto-wrap
2. Every pull quote gets its own amber thin rule above and below — never floated without visual grounding
3. Images always bleed to at least one edge — no white borders around photographs
4. Slide numbers in amber `#C9A96E`, set small (9pt equivalent), bottom-left, hidden on Slide 1
5. No decorative icons, no emoji, no bullet point symbols — use em-dash or plain line spacing to separate items
6. Dark slides (Slides 2, 6, 8) use off-white `#FFFBF0` for all text; light slides (Slides 3, 4, 5, 7) use soft black `#1A1A1A`

**OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS**
- 8 slides total
- 16:9 widescreen format
- Export-ready for Canva or Keynote
- Fonts embedded or substituted with exact equivalents
- Each slide composited as described — no template defaults

## 2. INSTAGRAM STORY SEQUENCE PROMPT

Create a cinematic, warm-documentary Instagram Story sequence for a Life & Self-Development post titled: "The Lie We Inherited About Strength"

**THE EMOTIONAL CORE**
The viewer should feel the specific texture of silence that looks like composure — the recognition of carrying something alone for so long it started to feel normal. And then, very quietly, permission to put it down.

**STYLE DIRECTION**
This sequence moves like a short film, not a carousel. Each frame is a held breath. Visual language is candid photography and spare typography — no decorative elements, no graphic flourishes, nothing that competes with the emotional weight of the words. Warm beige and deep shadow alternate throughout — the sequence literally moves from isolation into light as the frames progress. Film grain texture at 15–20% opacity over every frame. Ambient, intimate. The kind of story someone watches alone at 11 PM and screenshots the last frame.

**COLOR PALETTE**
Warm beige `#F5F0E8` · Amber gold `#C9A96E` · Muted brown `#8B6F5E` · Off-white `#FFFBF0` · Soft black `#1A1A1A`

**FORMAT**
- Vertical 9:16
- 8 frames
- Transition: slow cross-dissolve, 0.6s
- Film grain overlay: consistent across all frames

**STORY FLOW**

FRAME 1 — THE HOOK
- Visual: Close-up of a plaster arm cast — warm golden light, someone's hand visible, a few words written on the cast in black marker. Composition tight on the cast. Warm tones, full frame.
- Text: "Break a bone." — off-white `#FFFBF0`, large bold sans-serif, top-left of frame, 3 words only

FRAME 2 — THE CONTRAST
- Visual: A person alone at a kitchen table, late at night. One overhead light. Empty chair across from them. The cast is gone. The warmth is gone. Deep shadow in the corners. They're not looking at anything.
- Text: "Say you're drowning." — same position as Frame 1, same typeface. Below it, in smaller amber `#C9A96E`: "Room goes quiet."

FRAME 3 — THE MECHANISM NAMED
- Visual: Old furniture in a dim room, draped in fabric. Afternoon light from a partially closed window. The sense of things passed down, never examined. Slightly dusty quality to the light.
- Text: "We inherited this." — centered, off-white, large. Below in muted brown `#8B6F5E` italic: "Nobody chose it. It just got handed down."

FRAME 4 — THE PERSONAL
- Visual: A person at a table, looking at a phone. The phone screen shows a chat thread — just the visual suggestion of it, not readable. Their expression: composed on the surface, something else underneath.
- Text: "I called because I needed someone to say:" — amber, small, top of frame. Below it, larger and off-white: *"I hear you. You don't deserve this."* (italic)

FRAME 5 — THE ANALOGY
- Visual: Dark background `#1A1A1A`. Two doors, side by side, photographed simply — one open, one closed. That's all.
- Text: Left door label (small amber): "Personal trainer" · Right door label (small amber): "Therapist"
- Below, centered in off-white bold: "Same logic." — then in amber italic on the next line: "Completely different social response."

FRAME 6 — THE REFRAME
- Visual: Abstract close-up — two materials, one fractured and sharp at the edges, one bent and intact. Same amber tone. No labels.
- Text: Full-bleed frame. Top-left in off-white small caps: STRENGTH
- Center of frame, large, off-white bold: "Vulnerability isn't the opposite of strength."
- Bottom-right, amber italic: "It's proof of it."

FRAME 7 — THE OPENING
- Visual: A concrete wall, tight close-up. A single crack. Warm amber light pressing through from behind — subtle, unmistakable. The crack is not dramatic. It's the most important thing in the frame.
- Text: Minimal. Bottom-center, off-white, quiet: "One conversation at a time."

FRAME 8 — EMOTIONAL STILLNESS
- Visual: Two people in a warm interior — one has their hand on the other's forearm. Not a hug. Just contact. Candid. Neither is looking at the camera. Warm lamp light at the edge of frame. The image is slightly soft in focus.
- Text: Top of frame, amber small: @mistakenlyhuman
- Center of frame, off-white bold: "You don't have to carry this alone."
- Bottom of frame, muted brown italic small: "Start there."

**ANIMATION STYLE**
Slow dissolve between all frames. Text fades in 0.3s after image settles. No bounce, no slide, no pop. Stillness is the motion.

**TYPOGRAPHY**
Humanist bold sans-serif for hero statements. Clean regular weight for secondary lines. Italic only for quotes and emotional lines. Every frame: maximum 2 type elements. No paragraph copy. No full sentences beyond 10 words.

**GOAL**
After the last frame, the viewer should feel quietly seen — not lectured, not motivated, not inspired in the hollow sense. Seen. Like the sequence knew exactly where they were sitting when they watched it.

## 3. REEL COVER / THUMBNAIL PROMPT

Create a cinematic vertical reel cover (9:16) for a Life & Self-Development video titled: "The Lie We Inherited About Strength" — a personal essay on mental health stigma, male silence, and what real strength actually looks like.

**VISUAL DIRECTION**
A man seated alone at a kitchen table, late at night. Single overhead warm light — amber-gold pool illuminating the table surface, his hands, part of his face. Deep shadow fills the rest of the frame. The chair across from him is empty. He's not looking at anything specific — a middle distance. The composition is intimate, almost intrusive, the way a documentary still feels. This is not a sad stock photo. This is a specific human moment: someone who's been fine for too long. Shot from slightly above eye level, tight enough to feel close. Film grain texture at 18% opacity over the full frame.

**TEXT OVERLAY**
- Main text: THE LIE WE INHERITED ABOUT STRENGTH
  - Set in bold humanist sans-serif, off-white `#FFFBF0`, all caps
  - Positioned upper two-thirds of frame, left-aligned with generous left margin
  - Large enough to command the frame but not crowd the visual — approximately 14–16% of total frame height per line
  - 3-line break: THE LIE WE / INHERITED ABOUT / STRENGTH
- Sub text: "Silence isn't strength."
  - Amber gold `#C9A96E`, italic, approximately 40% the size of the main headline
  - Positioned directly below the headline, same left alignment
  - Thin amber rule above sub text, full width of the text block

**COLOR TREATMENT**
Warm beige base tones in the mid-frame. Soft black `#1A1A1A` in deep shadow corners — natural vignette, not applied. Amber gold `#C9A96E` used only in the text overlay accent rule and sub text. No color grading that pushes into blue or green — this cover stays warm even in its darkness. Film grain overlay at 18% opacity.

**TYPOGRAPHY**
- Headline: Bold weight, humanist sans-serif (e.g., DM Sans Bold or Inter ExtraBold), all caps, off-white `#FFFBF0`, letter-spacing slightly open (+2%)
- Sub text: Regular italic weight, same family, amber `#C9A96E`
- No drop shadow on text — the contrast between text and image must be achieved through image exposure control, not shadow effects
- Handle: @mistakenlyhuman in muted brown `#8B6F5E`, very small, bottom-right corner, regular weight

**MOOD**
The quiet before something breaks — not despair, but the specific exhaustion of a person who has been fine for too long and is starting to feel the weight of that word.

**OPTIMIZATION NOTE**
In a vertical feed, the deep shadow corners and single warm light source create natural visual tension that stops the scroll — the eye moves toward the light, finds the person, and the headline lands before they can keep scrolling. The large, high-contrast headline is readable as a thumbnail at 80px width. The sub text rewards the pause.
