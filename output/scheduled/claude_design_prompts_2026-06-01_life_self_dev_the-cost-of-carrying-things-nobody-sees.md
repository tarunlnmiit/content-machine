## 1. SLIDE DECK PROMPT

You are an expert presentation designer and visual storyteller creating a quiet, unsettling, emotionally precise slide deck for a Life & Self-Development blog post about invisible emotional weight.

**PROJECT CONTEXT**
- Topic: The Cost of Carrying Things Nobody Sees
- Tone: Quiet, honest, gently confrontational
- Audience: High-functioning people who suspect they're not as okay as they appear; people who've mastered the performance of fine; adults in their late 20s–40s carrying unresolved grief, relationship weight, or chronic emotional suppression

**VISUAL DIRECTION**
- Style: Warm cinematic documentary. Golden-hour and blue-hour photography. Minimal text per frame — every word has earned its place. High-end editorial stillness, like a long-form feature in The New Yorker illustrated edition. Generous negative space. Intimacy over impact. No motivational energy whatsoever.
- Color Palette: Warm beige `#F5F0E8` · Amber gold `#C9A96E` · Muted brown `#8B6F5E` · Off-white `#FFFBF0` · Soft black `#1A1A1A` · with one cool note of slate blue-grey `#8A9BB0` for cognitive/psychological slides
- Typography: Bold humanist sans-serif (e.g. Canela, Freight Display, or equivalent) for emotional headline statements. Clean body serif at small scale. Strong hierarchy: one dominant phrase per slide, supporting text small and restrained. No bullet styling — plain text, line-spaced at 1.6 minimum. Never center all elements — use deliberate offset.
- Imagery Direction: All photography must feel found, not staged. Solitary human figures in rooms with natural window light. Hands on keyboards with attention elsewhere. Two people in the same frame who are not connecting. Still-life moments: open journal and pen, morning light, untouched coffee. Outdoors at golden hour with hand on chest. NO stock-business imagery. NO bright environments. NO eye contact with camera. Every image should carry ambient sadness — not melodrama, just the quiet weight of being human.
- Animation/Transitions: Slow dissolves between slides (0.6s minimum). No builds within slides — elements arrive together or not at all. No flying text. No motion on images except a single, imperceptible slow zoom (Ken Burns, 8% scale over 30s). Silence as rhythm.

**HERO SLIDE**
- Title: The Cost of Carrying Things Nobody Sees
- Main Text (ALL CAPS): THE WEIGHT NOBODY MEASURES
- Sub Text: There's another kind of exhaustion — and it doesn't show up anywhere.
- Hero Visual Direction: Person seated alone near a large window, late afternoon light crossing them diagonally. They are not sad — they are still. No phone, no book. Eyes not visible or looking away from camera. The room behind them is tidy, warm, completely normal. The stillness is the point. Warm beige overlay at 18% opacity. Title in soft black bottom-left, generous margin.

**SLIDE STRUCTURE + CONTENT**

SLIDE 1 — "The Weight Nobody Sees"
- Not burnout from doing too much
- Exhaustion from holding too much
- No metric captures it
- Visual Direction: Full-bleed warm photography — a person at a window, distant. Bullet text rendered as three short lines, stacked left-aligned, generous spacing. No dividers. Amber gold `#C9A96E` used only for the slide heading.

SLIDE 2 — "The Cognitive Tax"
- Unresolved things run background processes
- Zeigarnik effect: the mind holds incomplete loops
- Emotional weight draws from the same pool as work
- Visual Direction: Close-up of hands slightly out of focus over a dim-lit keyboard. Screen glow is the only light source. Slate blue-grey `#8A9BB0` used as heading accent — the only cool note in the deck, signaling we're now inside psychology. Text on a semi-transparent `#1A1A1A` panel at 70% opacity, right side of frame.

SLIDE 3 — "What It Looks Like"
- Rereading the same paragraph three times
- Decisions feel heavier than they should
- Forty minutes pass with nothing to show
- Visual Direction: Single person at a desk, viewed from behind or side. Posture slightly collapsed. Room is lit but dim. These are the specific, familiar symptoms of invisible load — the visual should make viewers recognize themselves. Pull quote from blog floated above bullets: "You're not being lazy. You're running something heavy in the background." — in italic, slightly larger than body text.

SLIDE 4 — "It Doesn't Stay Invisible"
- Leaks into relationships without warning
- The half-second delay before warmth
- Silence has a texture people can feel
- Visual Direction: Two people at a table — both present, neither connecting. Street photography aesthetic, slightly underexposed. The gap between them is the subject. KEY QUOTE displayed at large scale across lower third: "Silence has a texture people can feel." — soft black on off-white `#FFFBF0` strip, full bleed width.

SLIDE 5 — "Why We Keep Carrying"
- Putting it down means looking at it first
- The weight becomes familiar — even safe
- Letting go can feel like betrayal
- Visual Direction: A person carefully folding something and placing it in a bag. Deliberate, quiet. Or: hands cupped around a warm mug, knuckles slightly visible, holding on. Warm amber tones. This slide is the most psychologically honest — the visual must not dramatize or romanticize, just show the tenderness of holding on.

SLIDE 6 — "The First Move"
- Not a framework. Not a breakthrough.
- Name it accurately — even only to yourself
- The loop needs attention, not resolution
- Visual Direction: Open journal on a wooden surface, blank page, single pen. Morning window light. The page is still empty — that is the point. Intention, not execution. Text positioned bottom-left with significant negative space above. Heading in amber gold.

SLIDE 7 — "Stop Calling It Nothing"
- You don't have to share it
- You don't have to resolve it today
- You have to let it be what it is: something that has weight
- Visual Direction: Person outdoors at golden hour, hand flat on chest, eyes closed. Not dramatic — still. Contact with something interior. Warm gold light. This is the closing image. KEY QUOTE full-bleed below the visual on a soft black `#1A1A1A` panel: "You are not as good at hiding it as you think. And even if you were — you deserve to put it down." — white text, generous padding, no decoration.

**DESIGN RULES**
1. Never use more than 25 words of copy on any single slide — the brevity is the message
2. Every photograph must contain a human figure; no pure abstract or still-life shots without human presence nearby
3. Amber gold `#C9A96E` is reserved for headings only — never used in body text or decoration
4. Slate blue-grey `#8A9BB0` appears only on Slide 2 (cognitive section) — cold note as intentional contrast
5. Text always sits in areas of visual quiet — never over the subject of the photograph
6. Final slide holds the longest pause — if animated, extend transition out to 1.2 seconds

**OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS**
16:9 widescreen format. 7 slides plus hero. Minimum 160pt for headline text. Export at 1920×1080. Consistent margin: 80px all sides. No logo except handle @mistakenlyhuman bottom-right of hero slide, 11pt, muted brown `#8B6F5E`.

## 2. INSTAGRAM STORY SEQUENCE PROMPT

Create a cinematic, emotionally grounded Instagram Story sequence for a Life & Self-Development post titled: The Cost of Carrying Things Nobody Sees

**THE EMOTIONAL CORE**
The viewer should feel seen — not inspired, not motivated. Seen. The specific, quiet recognition of someone who has been carrying something they've never named, and suddenly someone else has named it for them.

**STYLE DIRECTION**
Warm documentary photography with film grain. Golden-hour and interior window light. No bright environments. Every frame should feel like a still from a contemplative short film — the kind that lingers after you've closed your phone. Pacing is slow, deliberate. Silence between frames. No graphic elements except restrained typography. The aesthetic is intimate, not polished. Slightly underexposed is preferable to bright.

**COLOR PALETTE**
Warm beige `#F5F0E8` · Amber gold `#C9A96E` · Muted brown `#8B6F5E` · Off-white `#FFFBF0` · Soft black `#1A1A1A`

**FORMAT**
Vertical 9:16. 7 frames. Transitions: slow dissolve, 0.7s. Typography appears already on frame — no text animation within frames.

**STORY FLOW**

FRAME 1 — Opening Hook
- Visual: Person seated alone near a large window. Natural light crossing them diagonally. Late afternoon. Eyes not visible or facing away. The room behind is completely normal — nothing is wrong except the quality of their stillness. Film grain texture overlay at 12%.
- Text: "There's a version of you most people won't meet." — centered, bold humanist sans-serif, off-white `#FFFBF0`, 36pt, bottom third of frame

FRAME 2 — The Problem Named
- Visual: Close-up of hands hovering over a keyboard, slightly unfocused. Screen glow the only light source. Room dim. The work is happening, something else is too.
- Text: "Not burnout." / "Something quieter." — two lines, stacked, left-aligned, bold, soft black `#1A1A1A` on warm beige panel `#F5F0E8` at 85% opacity across bottom third

FRAME 3 — The Cognitive Cost
- Visual: Person at desk viewed from behind, slightly collapsed posture. The room is functional. They are too — barely. Warm, dim light.
- Text: "Forty minutes pass." / "Nothing to show." / "You're not lazy. You're running something heavy." — stacked lines, italic for the third line, off-white on dark overlay `#1A1A1A` at 65%

FRAME 4 — The Relational Leak
- Visual: Two people at a table. Not fighting. Not connecting. Both present, both somewhere else. Candid, street-photography aesthetic, slightly underexposed.
- Text: "Silence has a texture" / "people can feel." — two lines, large scale, amber gold `#C9A96E`, serif italic, centered, upper third

FRAME 5 — The Paradox
- Visual: Hands carefully, almost tenderly folding something. Or cupped around a warm mug. The gesture of holding on. Warm amber tones, close crop.
- Text: "Putting it down" / "means looking at it first." — centered, soft black `#1A1A1A`, bold sans-serif, generous line spacing, mid-frame

FRAME 6 — The First Move
- Visual: Open journal on a surface in morning light. Blank page. Pen beside it. The intention to write, before anything has been written.
- Text: "You don't have to share it." / "You don't have to resolve it today." — stacked, left-aligned, muted brown `#8B6F5E`, small humanist sans-serif, lower third — quiet, not commanding

FRAME 7 — Closing Stillness
- Visual: Same window as Frame 1, but light shifted slightly warmer — closer to golden hour. Person still present, posture fractionally more open. No dramatic change. Just one degree of ease.
- Text: "Stop calling it nothing." — single line, centered, large, off-white `#FFFBF0`, bold, lower third. Below it, very small: @mistakenlyhuman in muted brown `#8B6F5E`

**ANIMATION STYLE**
Long dissolves between frames, minimum 0.7s. No motion within frames except an imperceptible slow Ken Burns zoom (5% over the frame duration). No swipes, cuts, or energetic transitions. Pacing like turning pages in a quiet room.

**TYPOGRAPHY**
Primary: Bold humanist sans-serif (Canela, GT America, or equivalent). Secondary: Clean italic serif for pull quotes. Never more than 15 words per frame. Generous line spacing (1.7). No drop shadows — use panel overlays for legibility instead.

**GOAL**
After Frame 7, the viewer should feel: Someone finally said it. Not motivated to act immediately — just less alone in what they're carrying. That recognition is the entire point.

## 3. REEL COVER / THUMBNAIL PROMPT

Create a cinematic vertical reel cover (9:16) for a Life & Self-Development video about the invisible emotional weight people carry in silence.

**VISUAL DIRECTION**
A single person seated near a large interior window. The room is warm, tidy, ordinary — nothing visually wrong. Late afternoon light falls across them diagonally, illuminating their face in three-quarter profile. They are not looking at camera. Their posture reads as still rather than sad — present but elsewhere. The quality of their stillness is the subject of the image, not any visible distress. Film grain texture overlay at 10%. Slightly underexposed overall — the window is the brightest element. Warm beige and amber tones dominate; the person's clothing should be a muted earth tone, nothing bright.

**TEXT OVERLAY**
- Main text: THE WEIGHT NOBODY MEASURES — ALL CAPS, bold humanist sans-serif, off-white `#FFFBF0`, 52pt, left-aligned, lower third, 80px left margin
- Sub text: You're carrying something. This is what it costs. — sentence case, italic serif, muted brown `#8B6F5E`, 18pt, directly below main text, same left alignment

**COLOR TREATMENT**
Base: warm beige ambient tones from photography. Subtle warm amber `#C9A96E` gradient overlay from bottom — 0% opacity at mid-frame, 30% at very bottom to ensure text legibility without obscuring the image. No vignette. No hard panel behind text — rely on the gradient and image composition to keep text readable.

**TYPOGRAPHY**
Main text: heavy weight, tight tracking (-0.02em), no line break. Sub text: light weight, normal tracking, one line only. Both left-aligned, not centered. Text lives in the lower third where image naturally darkens.

**MOOD**
The cover should feel like the still frame before a film begins — quiet authority, not urgency. The kind of image that makes someone slow their scroll because they recognize something in it, not because it's visually loud.

**OPTIMIZATION NOTE**
In a vertical feed, this cover works because it refuses to compete for attention through brightness or motion. The stillness is the scroll-stop. The specific, slightly unsettling quality of the person's composure — present but not quite there — will cause viewers to pause to understand what they're looking at. The text confirms the feeling before they've read a word.
