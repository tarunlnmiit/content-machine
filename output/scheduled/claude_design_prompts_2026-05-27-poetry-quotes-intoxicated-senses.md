## 1. SLIDE DECK PROMPT

You are an expert presentation designer and visual storyteller creating a suspended, intimate, fine-art slide deck for a poetry and reflection piece about love as intoxication and the loss of internal coordinates.

**PROJECT CONTEXT**
- Topic: The Hangover That Won't Lift — A poem about love as intoxication, when the mind and heart stop agreeing
- Tone: intimate, suspended, precise
- Audience: People who have felt quietly occupied by someone — who recognize the dissonance between what their mind concludes and what their heart keeps doing; late-night readers who find meaning in verse; introspective creators aged 25–40 who trust poetry to name what prose can't

**VISUAL DIRECTION**
- Style: Fine art photography meets literary journal. Think Magnum Photos meets The Paris Review. No lifestyle polish, no motivational brightness. Each slide is a still from a film that hasn't been made yet — understated, heavy with presence. Compositions lean toward stillness, asymmetry, and negative space that breathes like a held pause. The deck should feel like reading alone at 2 AM, lamp on, the rest of the room dark.
- Color Palette: Deep indigo `#1C1635` as the dominant base — almost black but with violet depth. Antique gold `#C9A96E` for all typographic highlights and decorative elements. Cream `#FAF3E0` for body text. Dusty rose `#D4A5A5` as an occasional warmth tone for visual accents only. Soft charcoal `#3D3D4F` for secondary text.
- Typography: Primary headline font — an elegant literary serif (think Freight Display, Cormorant Garamond, or similar high-contrast editorial serif) set large with generous tracking. Poem lines rendered in refined italic serif, never bold, with preserved line breaks and 1.8× line height. Attribution and labels in refined small caps. Zero decorative fonts. Maximum 3 text elements per slide.
- Imagery Direction: As if briefing a fine art photographer — every image is a single, still, emotionally loaded scene. Candle flames soft-focused against total darkness. A moth in slow orbit, wings glowing amber at the edges. Ocean waves pulling back from shore in cool blue-grey morning light. Frost spreading across glass in geometric silence. A rainbow arching over an empty wet road. A figure from behind standing at a fog-covered cliff edge — no ground visible, no sky. Cold coffee on a desk at night, laptop open, steam gone. All images: low key, film-grain texture, no artificial vibrancy, no HDR sharpness. Mood — presence and weight, not beauty.
- Animation/Transitions: Long dissolves only (600–800ms). No slides, no fly-ins, no animated bullets. Text fades in as a single unit. Images dissolve into the next. Pacing is deliberately slow — let each frame hold before the next arrives.

**HERO SLIDE (Slide 1)**
- Title: The Hangover That Won't Lift
- Main Text: WHEN THE HEART TAKES OVER THE KEYS
- Sub Text: A poem about love that refuses to schedule its own departure
- Hero Visual Direction: A cold cup of coffee on a wooden desk, late at night. Laptop screen open in the background, faint code or text barely legible, casting a soft diffuse glow. The coffee cup center-frame, steam long gone. Warm amber desk lamp from the right, casting a long shadow left. The surrounding room is pure darkness. This is the moment after someone's voice replays in your head mid-work — you're present but not here. Shot at tabletop level, slightly wide, quiet documentary framing.

**SLIDE STRUCTURE + CONTENT**

SLIDE 2 — "It starts quietly"
- Exact copy: "One day you're fine." / "The next, someone's laugh is living rent-free in your head." / "The part of your brain that runs the show has quietly handed over the keys."
- Text placement: left-aligned, stacked vertically with generous spacing. Each line fades in sequence.
- Visual: A dark bedroom. Ceiling view — a single person lying awake, thin sheet, one arm across forehead, faint blue moonlight from a window just out of frame. Still life of private insomnia. Grain-heavy, blue-black with cream sheets.

SLIDE 3 — The Poem Opens (full stanza block)
- Exact copy (preserve line breaks, render in italic serif):
  *Love has taken away*
  *all my senses*
  *like that of alcohol*
  *effects of booze reside*
  *for a short period*
  *but your intoxication*
  *doesn't want to go away*
  *my heart keeps enjoying the hangover you created*
- Layout: Poem text centered, italic, generous vertical space. No image — pure deep indigo `#1C1635` background. A single thin antique gold `#C9A96E` rule above the poem, 40% width, centered. This is a reading slide — nothing competes with the words.

SLIDE 4 — "What makes it stay"
- Exact copy: "Vague longing is forgettable." / "But twinkling eyes. Ebony hair. An enlightening smile." / "That's a face. A real one."
- Visual: Extreme close-up of a candle flame in total darkness. The flame fills the center of the frame — warm amber-gold against absolute black, with slight motion blur suggesting a breath or a draft from somewhere unseen. No candle body visible. Just the burning.

SLIDE 5 — The Four Nature Comparisons (pull-quote cascade)
- Exact copy (render each as its own centered line in italic serif):
  *like a moth attracted to the flame*
  *like waves to the shore*
  *like cold to the winter*
  *like a rainbow after the rain*
- Visual: A moth in orbit around a candle flame — macro photography, wings translucent amber at the edges where the light catches them, the rest of the frame pure black. Shot from slightly above and behind the moth. No drama, no collision — just the perpetual circuit. This is physics, not choice.
- Text overlay: each line appears over the image sequentially, bottom-center, antique gold, small italic serif. Four lines stacked with spacing, or animated one at a time.

SLIDE 6 — The Collision
- Exact copy: "Earlier, mind and heart worked in sync." / "Then: *their ecosystems have collided.*" / "No ground to stand on. No sky to cover my head with."
- The middle line (italicized direct poem quote) is rendered notably larger than the surrounding lines — hierarchy that emphasizes the pivot.
- Visual: A figure standing alone at the edge of a fog-covered cliff. Shot directly from behind, centered. No visible ground below the edge. No sky above — only undifferentiated grey fog filling every quadrant except the immediate ground at their feet. The figure is small, still, unhurried. They are not falling. They are standing at a fault line and they know it.

SLIDE 7 — Not a Malfunction (closing reframe)
- Exact copy: "When the system is overturned, that's a signal." / "The only honest one you'll get that something actually matters." / "That's not a problem to solve. That's the experience itself."
- Visual: Return to the candle — this time a tight, meditative close-up of just the flame tip. Perfectly sharp in the center, warm-gold light diffusing into total darkness at the edges. The flame flickers almost imperceptibly. No moth now. Just the burning. Quiet and steady and unresolved.
- Sub-footer: "@breathofpoetry" in small caps, antique gold, bottom-right

**DESIGN RULES**
1. Never use more than 3 text elements per slide — headline, body, attribution. Strip everything else.
2. All poem text: italic serif only, never bold, always with 1.8× line height, line breaks preserved exactly as written
3. Gold `#C9A96E` is reserved for typographic highlights and the single ruling line only — never as a background fill
4. Images must be low-key and film-grain textured — no bright crisp digital photography, no HDR, no vibrant saturation
5. Negative space is load-bearing — do not fill it. A slide that is 70% deep indigo `#1C1635` and 30% type is correct
6. No slide should feel like a motivational poster — if a layout could appear on a Canva template, revise it

**OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS**
Produce 7 slides matching the above structure. Each slide must feel like a page from a literary art book, not a presentation template. Export at 1920×1080 widescreen, 16:9. Final deck should feel like a continuous immersive reading experience — slow, weighted, precise.

## 2. INSTAGRAM STORY SEQUENCE PROMPT

Create a cinematic fine-art Instagram Story sequence for a poetry post titled: *The Hangover That Won't Lift — when love quietly overrides your internal operating system*

**THE EMOTIONAL CORE**
The viewer should feel the specific, unasked-for weight of being occupied by someone — not dramatic heartbreak, but the quieter, more disorienting experience of realizing your own mind is no longer entirely yours. By the last frame, they should feel seen and strangely held by that recognition.

**STYLE DIRECTION**
Every frame is a still from a film that hasn't been released. The visual grammar is literary documentary — slow, composed, grain-textured. Nothing should feel like an Instagram template. The color story moves from cold deep indigo through warm amber-gold, then settles in muted suspension — no clean resolution, no brightness. Think the visual language of a Terrence Malick film intercut with a poetry journal: natural light, organic textures, human stillness. Typography is sparse. Some frames carry only 4 words. Silence is structural.

**COLOR PALETTE**
Deep indigo `#1C1635` · Antique gold `#C9A96E` · Cream `#FAF3E0` · Dusty rose `#D4A5A5` · Soft charcoal `#3D3D4F`

**FORMAT**
Vertical 9:16. 8 frames. Transitions: long cross-dissolves, 700ms minimum. No hard cuts. No swipe transitions.

**STORY FLOW**

FRAME 1 — The Hook
Visual: A cold cup of coffee on a dark wooden surface — night, laptop glow barely catching the rim, steam completely gone. Shot at table level, tight, no hands, no person. The coffee is evidence of a mind that drifted mid-task.
Text: "One day you're fine." — centered, cream `#FAF3E0`, elegant italic serif, large, middle of frame
Overlay: Slight film grain texture across entire frame

FRAME 2 — The Intoxication Named
Visual: Amber whiskey glass on its side, empty, golden residue pooling slightly on a dark surface. Soft focus, bokeh background. Single overhead warm light. The kind of still life that implies aftermath.
Text: *"your intoxication doesn't want to go away"* — italic serif, antique gold `#C9A96E`, centered, lower third
Subtext: none

FRAME 3 — The Specificity That Stays
Visual: Close-up of a candle flame, total surrounding darkness. The flame in absolute sharp focus, warm amber-gold, slight flicker caught mid-movement. Nothing else visible. No candle body. No surface. Just the burning against black.
Text: "my heart keeps enjoying / the hangover you created" — two-line italic serif, cream, centered, upper-middle zone

FRAME 4 — The Physics Begins
Visual: A moth in soft macro — wings translucent where amber light catches them at the edges, body faintly visible, the surrounding frame dark. The moth is mid-orbit, not landing, not fleeing. Just perpetually drawn.
Text: "like a moth / to the flame" — italic serif, cream `#FAF3E0`, centered, small, low in frame. Generous negative space above.

FRAME 5 — The Cascade of Nature
Visual: Ocean waves pulling back from shore in pre-dawn grey light. Blue-grey water, cool and indifferent, the foam retreating and then — off screen — you know it's already returning. No horizon visible. Just the cycle.
Text: "like waves to the shore / like cold to the winter / like a rainbow after the rain" — three-line stacked italic serif, antique gold `#C9A96E`, centered, lower half. Each line slightly smaller than the previous, suggesting an inevitable list.

FRAME 6 — The Collision
Visual: A figure from directly behind, standing at the edge of a fog-covered cliff. No ground below. No sky above. Pure grey suspension on every side. The figure is calm, still, small in frame — not desperate, just standing at a place where coordinates no longer apply.
Text: "No ground to stand on. / No sky to cover my head with." — italic serif, cream, centered, upper third. The frame's negative space carries the weight.

FRAME 7 — The Honest Admission
Visual: A still, dark room. Single desk lamp casting a warm circle on a closed journal and a pen set down beside it. The rest of the room: deep indigo shadow. This is 2 AM. This is the moment after writing something true.
Text: "the heart wants / what the heart wants" — italic serif, antique gold `#C9A96E`, centered, middle of frame. Larger than any previous text. This is the stillpoint.

FRAME 8 — The Close
Visual: Return to the candle flame from Frame 3 — now slightly wider, catching just the top edge of the candle body. The flame is completely steady this time. Burning in silence.
Text: "Some people just become / part of your physics." — italic serif, cream, centered, middle frame. Below it, in small refined caps: @breathofpoetry · breathofpoetry.substack.com

**ANIMATION STYLE**
Long dissolves only between every frame. Text fades in after the image settles — always. Pacing is deliberately unhurried. Each frame should hold for at least 4–5 seconds before transitioning. The sequence reads like turning pages in the dark.

**TYPOGRAPHY**
Primary: high-contrast literary serif italic (Cormorant Garamond Italic, Freight Display Italic, or equivalent). All poem lines: italic. All reflection lines: italic. No bold weights anywhere. No sans-serif. No decorative scripts.

**GOAL**
After the final frame, the viewer should feel the precise, aching recognition of being unable to navigate around someone — and instead of shame in that, a strange, quiet solidarity. The sequence should move something in them, not inform them.

## 3. REEL COVER / THUMBNAIL PROMPT

Create a cinematic vertical reel cover (9:16) for a poetry piece titled: *The Hangover That Won't Lift* — a poem about love as intoxication and the moment the mind and heart stop agreeing.

**VISUAL DIRECTION**
A macro close-up of a moth in slow orbit around a candle flame — shot from slightly above and behind the moth. The moth's wings are translucent amber-gold at the outermost edges where the light reaches, darker and textured at the body. The candle flame occupies the right-center of the frame, sharp, warm, living. The moth is mid-arc — not close enough to burn, not far enough to be free. The entire background is absolute darkness: no surface, no wall, no context. Just the moth, the flame, and the dark they share. Fine film grain texture across the entire image. No HDR, no digital sharpness — this looks like a still from a film, developed with intention.

**TEXT OVERLAY**
Main text: "The Hangover That Won't Lift" — elegant literary serif, Title Case, cream `#FAF3E0`, centered horizontally, positioned in the upper third of the frame. Large enough to read at thumbnail scale — approximately 14% of frame height. Generous letter-spacing.
Sub text: *when the heart wants what it wants* — italic serif, antique gold `#C9A96E`, centered, directly beneath the main title, approximately 40% the size of the main text. Soft — it whispers rather than declares.

**COLOR TREATMENT**
Base image: deep indigo-black shadows, warm amber-gold flame glow, translucent amber at moth wing edges. No color grading applied artificially — the scene's own light palette is the palette. Subtle dark vignette at all four corners, heaviest at bottom, drawing the eye toward the flame.

**TYPOGRAPHY**
Main title: high-contrast literary serif (Cormorant Display, Freight Display, or equivalent) — not bold weight, medium weight with natural contrast. Cream on dark: maximum legibility.
Sub text: matching serif italic, antique gold.
Handle: @breathofpoetry — small refined caps, antique gold `#C9A96E`, bottom-center, below sub text, 20% opacity lower. Present but not demanding.

**MOOD**
The quiet physics of being drawn — knowing you're circling something that won't end well, and choosing to orbit anyway because the light is too specific to leave.

**OPTIMIZATION NOTE**
The extreme low-key contrast (near-black background, single warm light source) creates instant visual separation in a vertical feed of bright thumbnails. The moth in-motion against darkness is scroll-stopping because it's biologically incongruous — unexpected, quiet, specific. The title in large cream serif is readable at 60px thumbnail size. The amber-gold flame glow creates warmth that pulls the eye before the mind processes the image.
