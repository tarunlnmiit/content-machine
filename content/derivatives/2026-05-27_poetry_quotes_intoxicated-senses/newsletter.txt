Subject: The hangover that refuses to lift
Preview: Some intoxications don't schedule a checkout time.

There's a kind of madness that doesn't announce itself.

One day you're fine. The next, someone's laugh is living rent-free in your head, and the part of your brain that's supposed to run the show has quietly handed over the keys.

I wrote a poem about this — about the moment love starts feeling less like a decision and more like physics. About twinkling eyes and ebony hair that stay specific and vivid when vague longing would've faded long ago.

About the moth that doesn't choose the flame. It's just wired toward light.

The sharpest image in the piece: no ground to stand on, no sky to cover your head with. Not chaos. Not obsession. Just the sudden, disorienting absence of coordinates.

I've been there. My guess is you have too.
