Subject: A poem that doesn't flinch (and why I wrote it)
Preview: The masked world doesn't just ignore genuine people. It goes after them.

This week I published something I'd been sitting on for a while — a poem about what it actually looks like when authenticity gets punished.

Not a motivational take. Not a framework. Just a clear-eyed look at a pattern most people recognize but few name directly: the world rewards the performance and goes after the real.

The insight that keeps staying with me: the ones tearing down genuine faces aren't confident — they're defended. Defense needs authenticity to disappear, because honesty is an indictment of performance. That's not accidental cruelty. It's structural.

I also wrote about what this does to creators specifically — the moment you realize the costume grows faster than the work, and the decision that forces on you.

No fix at the end. Just a name for the thing. Sometimes that's enough.
