The internet says it wants authenticity. Watch what it actually does to the people who show up that way:

People who write real things — real grief, real questions, real failure — often get overlooked. A louder, cleaner, more performative version of the same idea spreads everywhere. Not because it's truer. Because it's easier to consume.

I wrote a poem about this. One line doesn't soften it: 'shitting all over the hearts of the good ones.' That's what it actually feels like. No metaphor borrowed to make it safer. Just the thing itself.

The good ones aren't naive. They're just not willing to trade their interior for applause. And the world has built a thousand polished ways to punish that.

Here's what cuts deepest: the ones tearing down real faces are themselves masked. They're not confident — they're defended. There's a difference. Confidence doesn't need to flatten anything. Defense does.

So the masked world doesn't just ignore genuine people. It goes after them. Specifically. Deliberately. 'Ready to tear down the true faces.' Not by accident. Ready.

The motive is always the same: 'Only to make themselves happy. Only to make themselves happy.' Not righteousness. Not values. Just hunger wearing borrowed language about love and life.
