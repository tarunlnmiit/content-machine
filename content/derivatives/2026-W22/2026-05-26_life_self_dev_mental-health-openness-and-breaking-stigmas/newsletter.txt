Subject: The strength we inherited was wrong
Preview: And the people who passed it down paid for it too.

Break a bone, people sign your cast. Tell someone you haven't slept in weeks because your chest feels like it's caving in — and the room goes quiet.

I wrote about the definition of strength we all inherited. The one that says handle it yourself, show no cracks, keep moving. The one that turns inward when there's nowhere else for it to go — rerouting into anger, silence, and relationships that erode from the inside.

In this piece I go through why therapy still carries stigma when personal trainers don't. What happens when we confuse emotional support with problem-solving. And a specific late-night conversation with a friend that made me stop mid-sentence and realize: some moments don't need a mechanic. They need a witness.

The wall of silence doesn't fall at once. But it's already cracking.
