Some people just become part of your physics.

There's a hangover that has nothing to do with alcohol.

It's the one that stays. The one that doesn't schedule a checkout time.

Moth to flame. Waves to shore. Cold to winter. Rainbow after rain.

Some things don't choose. They just pull.

This poem is about that moment — when the mind and heart stop working in sync and you're left without a ground to stand on or a sky to cover your head with.

Not a problem to solve. The experience itself.
