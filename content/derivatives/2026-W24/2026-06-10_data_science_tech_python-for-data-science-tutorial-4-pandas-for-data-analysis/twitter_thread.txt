6 Pandas operations every data scientist actually uses daily (not the ones in tutorials):

Thread 👇

1/ isnull().sum()

Run this before anything else. Missing data in a filter column silently removes rows — your analysis looks complete but isn't. Know your nulls before you start.

2/ pd.to_numeric(errors='coerce')

Instead of crashing on bad values, this turns them into NaN and keeps your row structure intact. Especially useful when revenue columns have strings like 'approx 50k' hiding in them.

3/ .query()

Instead of:
df[(df['region'] == 'North') & (df['revenue'] > 5000)]

Write:
df.query("region == 'North' and revenue > 5000")

Three or more conditions? Always switch to .query().

4/ .agg() with named aggregations

region_summary = df.groupby('region').agg(
    total_revenue=('revenue', 'sum'),
    avg_order=('revenue', 'mean'),
    order_count=('order_id', 'count')
).reset_index()

Clean column names. Multiple metrics. One pass.

5/ .drop_duplicates(subset=['order_id'])

Duplicates in source data are more common than you think. Specifying subset= means you're deduplicating on the key that actually matters, not all columns.

6/ .pivot_table()

Grouped DataFrames are for computation. Pivot tables are for communication. When someone asks 'show me revenue by region and month' — this is the function that produces exactly what they're picturing.

The raw data doesn't tell you anything. Summaries do.
