I spent two weeks on my first real dataset before I ran a single line of analysis.

Not two weeks learning. Two weeks just trying to get the data into a usable state — column names with trailing spaces, dates formatted three different ways in the same column, revenue figures mixed with strings like 'approx 50k.'

That experience taught me something most Pandas tutorials skip: data cleaning isn't preparation for analysis. It is analysis. You can't separate the two.

The mental model that changed everything for me was simple: a DataFrame isn't a spreadsheet. It's a structured, labeled, query-able object. Once I understood that a column is just a NumPy array with a name, operations like boolean filtering and groupby stopped feeling like syntax to memorize and started feeling inevitable.

I've just published Tutorial 4 in my Python for Data Science series — Pandas for Data Analysis. It covers the four skills I return to on every project: exploring a new dataset in the first 60 seconds, selecting and filtering with .loc and .query(), cleaning messy data without losing row structure, and aggregating with .groupby() and .pivot_table().

If you're working with data and still spending more time on logistics than thinking — this is the tutorial that changes that ratio.
