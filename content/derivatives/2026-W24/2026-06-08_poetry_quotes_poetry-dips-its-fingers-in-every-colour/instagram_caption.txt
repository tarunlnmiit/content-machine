I'm a data scientist. I cry at poems. I've stopped pretending those are separate things.

Poetry doesn't stay in its lane.

It shows up in the code you wrote at 2 AM. In the smell of burnt toast on a Monday morning. In the grief that arrived when a number on a screen told you something you weren't ready for.

For years I treated data science and poetry as separate identities.

Then I noticed the same curiosity drove both — understanding what people feel beneath what they say.

Poetry dips its fingers in every colour. The ordinary. The technical. The unbearable.

It was never waiting for an invitation. It was already there.
