Subject: The poem that was already in your commute
Preview: It finds data scientists too. Here's where poetry actually shows up.

I have spent most of my career working with things that can be measured.

Data. Models. Systems. Dashboards. The clean grammar of proof.

And then, occasionally, a poem finds the exact nerve no dataset ever mapped, and something releases. I did not plan for that. I do not have a framework for it.

This week I wrote about where poetry actually finds you — not in literature classes or soft-cover journals, but in the code written at 2 AM, the burnt toast on a Monday morning, the grief that insists on specificity long after the facts have been accepted.

Poetry dips its fingers in every colour. It always has.

The only question is whether you notice when it arrives.
