Most people think poetry belongs in literature classes and soft-cover journals.

It doesn't.

It finds data scientists at 2 AM. It lives in burnt toast and bad commutes. It shows up at the funeral.

Here's where poetry actually finds you:

It finds you in the technical.

There's a reason programmers call elegant code beautiful. The best commit messages carry something real in them sometimes.

Poetry was already in the server room. You just hadn't noticed it.

It finds you in the ordinary.

Monday, 7:14 AM. Toast burns. You scrape it over the sink.

Poetry isn't elevated experience. It's ordinary experience, seen clearly for one second.

The grey commute counts. The blue of a Tuesday when nothing happens and you feel, inexplicably, okay.

It finds you in grief.

You don't miss "a person." You miss how they said your name. The exact pitch of their laugh at something stupid.

Poetry holds that specificity without resolving it. Doesn't ask grief to become a lesson. That is not a small mercy.

It finds you when you stop looking.

The times it's landed hardest — I was tired. Half-reading. Distracted by something smaller.

Poetry comes sideways. In a lyric you've heard forty times that suddenly means something different. In a sentence you'll carry for years without knowing why.

I spent years treating data science and poetry as separate identities.

One used numbers. The other used metaphor.

The tools were different. The curiosity was the same.

It took a while to stop pretending those two lives were separate.

There's no lesson here. That's the point.

Poetry doesn't sharpen your frameworks. It doesn't solve problems.

It makes you a person who has stood inside a colour, briefly, and recognized it for what it was.

That is not a small thing. It is the whole thing.

If a line has stayed with you — something read years ago that still lives in your chest — I'd love to hear it.

One line. That's the whole ask.
