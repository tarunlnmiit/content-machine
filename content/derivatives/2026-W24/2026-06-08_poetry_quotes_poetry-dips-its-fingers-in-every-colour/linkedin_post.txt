For most of my career I worked with things that can be measured — and then a poem found the exact nerve no dataset ever mapped.

I spent years treating data science and poetry as separate identities. Work that rewards precision. Work that rewards something else entirely.

Then I noticed the same thing drove both: the desire to understand what people actually feel beneath what they say. One pursuit used numbers. The other used metaphor. The tools were different. The curiosity was the same.

Poetry doesn't stay in its lane. It shows up in a line of code you wrote at 2 AM that reads like confession. In the grief that arrived when a number on a screen told you something true you weren't ready for. In the smell of burnt toast on a Monday morning when nothing is remarkable and everything, briefly, is.

I used to think this was a contradiction worth hiding. A data scientist who reads poetry, who sometimes cries at lines on a page.

Now I think the boundary between technical and lyrical was always porous. The best scientific papers have rhythm. The question is whether you notice when poetry arrives.

It doesn't need a wide-open door. A crack is enough.
