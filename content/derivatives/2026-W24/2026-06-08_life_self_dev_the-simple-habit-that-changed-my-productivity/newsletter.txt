Subject: 7 habits that quietly rewired my productivity
Preview: None of them require an app. Most take under five minutes.

I spent years building elaborate productivity systems. Second brain, time-block manifesto, weekly reviews with sub-reviews. I was busy. I was organized. I wasn't really moving.

What changed wasn't a new system. It was stripping back to seven habits simple enough to do on bad days.

The highest-impact one surprised me: how you end the day. A four-minute shutdown ritual — scan what's open, note where you'll pick it up tomorrow, say 'shutdown complete' out loud — gives your nervous system permission to stop. Better sleep is one result. The bigger one is starting the next morning already knowing where to begin.

The other six follow the same logic: not optimization, but contact. With how you're spending attention. With what you actually did. With what matters this week.

Productivity systems fail when life gets imperfect. These habits ask for honest, not perfect.
