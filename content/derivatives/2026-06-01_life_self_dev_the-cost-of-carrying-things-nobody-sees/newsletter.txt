Subject: The weight nobody can see
Preview: It's not burnout. It's something older and quieter — and it's costing you more.

There's a kind of exhaustion that has nothing to do with how much you're doing.

It comes from what you're holding. The grief without a name. The argument that ended without closure. The version of something that happened that only you witnessed.

Unresolved emotional weight runs background processes in your mind — drawing from the same cognitive pool as your actual work. Which explains the fog. The decisions that feel heavier than they should. The forty minutes that pass with nothing to show.

I've been there. Functioning from the outside. Exhausted from the inside. Not quite able to name why.

This week's piece is about that invisible load — what it costs in your work, your relationships, your ability to be present in the life in front of you. And the one move that has to happen before anything else can change.

It's not a resolution. It's a shift in how you hold what you're carrying.
