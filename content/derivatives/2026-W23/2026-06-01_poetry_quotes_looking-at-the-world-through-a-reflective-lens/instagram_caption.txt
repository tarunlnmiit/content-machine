The world says it wants authenticity. Watch what it does to the people who actually show up that way.

I wrote this from a quiet place.

Not rage. Clarity.

The good ones aren't naive — they just refuse to trade their interior for applause.

And the world has found a thousand polished ways to punish that.

This poem is for anyone who's felt worn down by the performance.
