# Permission to Write Your First Honest Lines — Worksheet

**Objective:** By the end of this worksheet, you will have written 3 honest lines — unedited, unpolished, yours.

---

## 1. The Noticing Exercise (Observation Layer)

Look around you right now — your room, your desk, out the window. Pick one small, specific thing you can see.

Write it down exactly as it is. Not what it means. Not what it reminds you of. Just what it *is*.

```
I see: _______________________________________________
```

Now add one detail that someone else might not notice about it:

```
What I notice that others might miss: _______________________________________________
```

---

## 2. The One True Sentence

Think of something you've felt recently that you haven't said out loud to anyone — not because it's shameful, but because it felt too small, or too strange, or too hard to explain.

Write one sentence that is completely true. Not poetic. Not impressive. Just true.

```
One true thing: _______________________________________________
```

Read it back. Does it feel like *you* wrote it, or like you wrote what you thought you should write? If it's the second one, try again below:

```
Truer version: _______________________________________________
```

---

## 3. The Honest Line — First Attempt

Take your true sentence from Task 2. Now break it into two or three lines — just by reading it aloud slowly and hearing where you naturally pause.

Don't rhyme. Don't make it pretty. Just break it where it breathes.

```
Line 1: _______________________________________________

Line 2: _______________________________________________

Line 3 (optional): _______________________________________________
```

## 4. The Constraint Exercise — Remove the Explanation

Rewrite your lines from Task 3, but this time: **cut every word that explains how you feel.** No "I was sad," no "it felt like," no "I realized." Only keep the image, the action, the fact.

*Example — Before:* "I felt lonely sitting on the roof at night"
*Example — After:* "Roof. Night. Just me and the water tank."

```
Your lines with explanations removed:

_______________________________________________
```
