## Part 3 — Building the Trigger

A habit without a trigger is just an intention. A trigger is a specific moment that *automatically* cues your habit. It follows this structure:

> **After/Before I ___ [existing routine], I will ___ [your habit] for ___ [duration].**

**5.** List three things you already do every day without fail (examples: wake up, brush teeth, eat lunch, check your phone after school, sit on the bus):

- ____
- ____
- ____

**6.** Now pair your keystone habit from Part 2 with one of the above. Write your trigger sentence:

> After / Before I ____, I will ____ for ____ minutes.

Read it aloud. Does it feel realistic for a Wednesday when you have exams the next day? If not, adjust the duration.
