### TWITTER THREAD

Everyone talks about finding someone safe. Or finding someone who makes you feel alive. They're wrong.

The real thing? Finding someone who does both at the same time.

Safety alone gets suffocating. You stop growing. You become smaller because you're protected from the very things that teach you who you are.

Aliveness without safety is reckless. It's excitement without roots. You burn bright and burn out, leaving nothing but ash and regret.

But the person who makes you feel safe AND alive? They do something different. They hold you steady while you expand. Your nervous system relaxes. Your presence grows. Both at once.

That's the specificity the poem gets right. It's not abstract love. It's the moment you imagine them sitting with your mama. Being fully themselves in your childhood home. And you watching it all happen.

That's when you stop dividing yourself. You stop needing permission from different parts of your life to love someone. You just love them, entire.

And they melt in your arms out of sheer love and embarrassment, knowing they finally belong everywhere you belong.

Safe and alive isn't a choice. It's a non-negotiable. If you've found that, hold it tight. If you're still looking, don't settle for either one alone.

### LINKEDIN POST

The person you're meant to build a life with isn't the one who makes you smaller—they're the one who makes you grow while holding you steady.

I spent years thinking safety and aliveness were opposing forces. You either found someone comfortable and watched yourself shrink, or you found someone exciting and lived in constant risk.

Then I read a poem that changed how I see love entirely.

It's the moment when someone you love lights up talking about your family. When you imagine them sitting with your mama, being fully themselves, belonging to your world without reservation. That's when you realize: real love doesn't ask you to choose between safety and growth. It demands both.

Your nervous system relaxes. Your presence expands. Your circles collapse into one circle. You stop dividing yourself across different relationships because this person is welcome everywhere.

That's not romance. That's integration. That's the dream you didn't know you were allowed to have.
