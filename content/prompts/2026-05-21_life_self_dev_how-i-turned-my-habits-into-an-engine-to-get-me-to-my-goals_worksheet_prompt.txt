Create a reflective worksheet PDF (A4, portrait) for "Map Your Habit Engine: Build a System of Connected Behaviors That Drive Real Goals"

Layout:
- Header (top 15%): Soft gradient background. Title + subtitle about personal growth.
- 5 sections vertically stacked, each with:
  - Section title (warm orange or teal accent)
  - Reflective prompt in italics
  - Blank space for handwritten response (30-50% of section height)
  - Dividing line between sections

Colors: Warm cream background (#FFFBF0), teal accents (#0D9488), dark gray text

Sections:
1. Audit Your Current Habits
   List 5–7 habits you practice regularly. For each, mark whether it's Foundation (sleep, movement, nutrition), Work (deep focus, iteration), or Reflection (journaling, review). Then ask: is this habit isolated, or does it connect to another habit on your list? Which ones feel disconnected?

2. Find Your Momentum Points
   Pick one Work habit you care about. Trace backward: which Foundation habit creates the conditions for it? Trace forward: how could its output feed into a Reflection habit? Write one specific momentum connection (example: 'Morning meditation → sharper thinking during deep work → clarity in evening review').

3. Spot Your Discipline Drains
   Which 2–3 habits feel like pure willpower to maintain? For each ask: Is this connected to something I deeply care about? Or does it sit alone? What would need to change for this to feel like it *makes sense* instead of feels forced? Where is discipline covering for poor architecture?

4. Design Your Feedback Loop
   An engine works because you can *see* it working. Pick one goal. What Reflection habit will show you if your Foundation and Work habits are actually supporting it? (Not just logging meditation minutes, but: are you clearer? Faster? More focused?) Design one simple feedback loop that proves the system matters.

5. Plan Your First Iteration
   Pick ONE habit that feels disconnected or forced. Remove it, reconnect it to something you care about, or restructure when/how you do it. What's the smallest change you can test this week? The system evolves — what's your first adjustment?
