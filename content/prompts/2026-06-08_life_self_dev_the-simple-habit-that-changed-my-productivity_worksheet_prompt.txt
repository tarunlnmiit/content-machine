Create a reflective worksheet PDF (A4, portrait) for "Your Productivity Reboot: Choosing and Implementing One Simple Habit"

Layout:
- Header (top 15%): Soft gradient background. Title + subtitle about personal growth.
- 5 sections vertically stacked, each with:
  - Section title (warm orange or teal accent)
  - Reflective prompt in italics
  - Blank space for handwritten response (30-50% of section height)
  - Dividing line between sections

Colors: Warm cream background (#FFFBF0), teal accents (#0D9488), dark gray text

Sections:
1. Diagnosis: Which Productivity Gap Are You Actually Experiencing?
   - Do you find yourself physically done with work but mentally still spinning on it? If yes, Shutdown Ritual might be your entry point.
   - Are you forgetting ideas mid-thought, or losing track of what needs to happen? Brain Dump could be the reset you need.
   - Do you start each day overwhelmed by everything, unsure which thing matters most? Eating the Frog (one only) might be your starting habit.
   - Which of these resonates most: lack of closure, decision fatigue, mental clutter, unfinished loops in your head, or no visibility of what you've actually accomplished?

2. Your Chosen Habit: Specific Implementation Plan
   - Which ONE habit from the blog will you start with this week? Name it and write it down — this is your commitment.
   - What does this habit look like in your actual day? Be specific: the time, the place, and the tool (pen and paper only, per the blog's philosophy).
   - What's the smallest version of this habit you could do today? Example: for Shutdown Ritual, just 2 minutes. For Brain Dump, one page.
   - What will stop you from doing this? (Honesty required: friction, forgetfulness, or doubt?) How will you remove that one barrier?

3. Your First Week: Daily Contact Points
   - Each day Monday through Sunday: Did you do your habit? What did you notice about your attention, your mood, or how you felt afterward?
   - The core insight from the blog: Each habit creates a moment of *contact* with how you're spending your attention. Where did you feel that contact this week? Be specific.

4. Define Your One Metric for This Week
   - Based on your chosen habit, what ONE number matters right now? Examples: shutdown rituals completed, brain-dump pages written, deepwork blocks finished, top-three lists written the night before, items on your done list, or weeks you actually tracked something.
   - Write down your metric and your realistic target for this week. (5 out of 7 days is a solid starting goal.)
   - End-of-week reflection: Did you hit your metric? What does that tell you about whether this habit is the right starting point for you?
