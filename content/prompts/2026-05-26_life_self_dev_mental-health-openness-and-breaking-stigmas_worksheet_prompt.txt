Create a reflective worksheet PDF (A4, portrait) for "Breaking the Lie: Where You Learned That Struggle Means Weakness"

Layout:
- Header (top 15%): Soft gradient background. Title + subtitle about personal growth.
- 5 sections vertically stacked, each with:
  - Section title (warm orange or teal accent)
  - Reflective prompt in italics
  - Blank space for handwritten response (30-50% of section height)
  - Dividing line between sections

Colors: Warm cream background (#FFFBF0), teal accents (#0D9488), dark gray text

Sections:
1. The Inheritance Inventory
   - Finish this sentence: The version of strength I learned growing up said...
   - Who taught you that asking for help was weakness? Was it explicit or just... understood?
   - What's one thing you've never told anyone because it felt like admitting defeat?

2. The Cost of Silence — What Has This Cost You?
   - When you've carried something alone, what happened to your relationships? To your sleep? To how you showed up?
   - Describe a moment when the 'I'm fine' response made things worse instead of better.
   - What would change if you named out loud what you're actually struggling with?

3. Practice: Creating Space for Someone Else
   - Think of someone you sense is struggling but hiding. Write the exact question you'd ask them — the one that actually invites honesty, not just a quick fix.
   - What would it look like to 'shut up and wait' after asking? (No advice. No platitudes. Just presence.)
   - Who in your life needs permission to not be okay right now?

4. Your Own Unspoken Truth
   - If shame and judgment weren't in the room — if no one would ever know — what would you admit about where you are right now?
   - What specifically are you carrying alone that deserves to be shared?
   - Who is one person (therapist, friend, family) you could tell first?

5. One Small Act of Breaking the Pattern
   - This week, choose: either tell someone one true thing you've been hiding, or create space for someone else to do so.
   - What's the actual first step? (Not the perfect conversation. The awkward text. The therapy appointment. The 'hey, I'm not doing great.')
   - What will you do when the discomfort of honesty hits?
